How To Play Tennis?

  1. Essential Gear

    2. Before you swing, you need the right tools.

    • The Racket: As a beginner, look for an "oversized" head. It provides a larger "sweet spot," making it easier to hit the ball.
    • The Shoes: Wear tennis-specific shoes if possible. They have flat soles designed for lateral (side-to-side) movement and won't scuff the court.
    • The Balls: Use standard yellow pressurized balls. If you find the game too fast at first, "green dot" balls are slightly depressurized and bounce slower.

  2. Basic Gameplay & Scoring

    3. The goal is to hit the ball over the net and into the opponent's court boundaries.

    • The Bounce: The ball can only bounce once on your side before you must hit it back.
    • The Lines: If the ball touches any part of the line, it is "in."
    • Scoring: Tennis has a unique "love" system:
    • 0 points = Love
    • 1 point = 15
    • 2 points = 30
    • 3 points = 40
    • 4 points = Game
    • Note: If it's 40-40, it's called "Deuce," and someone must win by two consecutive points.

  3. The Core Strokes

    4. Mastering these three shots will allow you to play a full match.

    1. The Forehand
      • This is usually the strongest shot for beginners.
      • Pivot: Turn your body sideways as the ball approaches.
      • Backswing: Take the racket back early.
      • Low to High: Swing from below the ball and finish with the racket over your opposite shoulder.
    2. The Backhand
      • Used when the ball comes to your non-dominant side.
      • Two-Handed: Most beginners find it easier to use two hands for extra stability and power.
      • Step In: Step toward the ball with your dominant foot as you swing.
    3. The Serve
      • Every point starts with a serve from behind the baseline.
      • The Toss: Toss the ball high enough so you can reach up and hit it at full extension.
      • The Target: You must hit the ball into the service box diagonally opposite to you.

  4. Beginner Tips for Success

    • Keep Your Eye on the Ball: It sounds cliché, but many beginners look at where they want the ball to go instead of the ball itself.
    • Split Step: Do a tiny hop on the balls of your feet right as your opponent hits the ball. This keeps your muscles "loaded" and ready to move.
    • Aim for Height: Don't try to skim the net. Aiming half a metre above the net gives you a safety margin and helps the ball land deep in the court.
